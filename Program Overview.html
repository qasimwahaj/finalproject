Program Overview

The International Rescue Committee (IRC) is a humanitarian organization founded in 1933 to provide relief and support to refugees and other displaced individuals around the world. The IRC provides a range of programs and services, including health care, education, and economic development support, to help individuals and communities recover and rebuild after conflict or disaster (IRC, n.d). One of the services provided by the IRC across the world is career development and readiness services. This programs might differ across different locations. This program evaluation is primarily based on the career development program provided at the IRC’s Atlanta office. However, this model can be used to measure the impact of similar career readiness programs designed for refugee population in the United States. The purpose of the IRC’s career development program is to support refugees in building their skills, knowledge, and experience to succeed in the U.S. workforce. The program is designed to provide career advising, job training, vocational education and other support to help participants achieve their career goals and improve their economic stability. While the program is expected to provide many positive outcomes for refugee clients, this impact evaluation design is interested on the economic empowerment impact of the program since the Career Development Program is run under the Economic Empowerment Program (IRC-RITA, n.d.).

While the Early Employment Program at IRC assist clients with basic or no English proficiency, the Career Development program assists clients who have at least intermediate English proficiency and familiarity with computer and technological tools. We determine eligibility through our English Proficiency test and our intake survey. The purpose of the career development program is to support refugees in building their skills, knowledge, and experience to succeed in the U.S. workforce. The program provides career advising, job training, vocational education, and other support to help participants achieve their career goals and improve their economic stability. The career development program is designed to provide comprehensive support to refugees, addressing not only their immediate job search needs but also their long-term career goals. Career coaches work with clients to develop personalized career plans, identify job opportunities, and provide training on job search strategies, interview skills, and workplace communication. Additionally, the program provides vocational training opportunities in fields that align with clients’ skills and interests, as well as job placement assistance. The Career Development Program primarily is primarily consisting of two components:

1. Career Counseling

Each client will be assigned a career coach who will work with them one-on-one to develop a career plan, set goals, and identify potential job opportunities. Clients will receive training on resume writing, job search strategies, interview skills, and workplace communication. Additionally, the program provides job placement assistance. The program will work with local employers to identify job openings and facility ate job placements for clients who have completed the job readiness training and career coaching components.

2. Vocational Training

Clients will have the opportunity to participate in vocational training programs that align with their skills and interests in areas such as data analytics, nursing, dental assistance and information technology. The IRC Atlanta partners with several educational institutions. They also assist refugee clients with their application as well as provide tuition assistance on a case-by-case basis and based on the availability of funding.

3. Employer Engagement

The career development program cultivates partnerships with employers to support clients in securing employment opportunities and addresses any issues that may arise. This relationship enables the career development team to provide the necessary support for clients to succeed in their jobs and for employers to effectively manage a diverse workforce.
